# 06 — Sizing: FSMA 204 KDE / CTE Capture (C7)

## Reference TAM

- Food traceability software NA: directional $400–800M total opportunity through FSMA 204 buildout cycle, with peak demand 2026–2028. Trustwell, FoodLogiQ, Wherefour, FoodReady, ReposiTrak, iTradeNetwork are the established players.
- FSMA 204 covered entities (FTL items): tens of thousands of NA establishments across produce, seafood, cheese, eggs, leafy greens, ready-to-eat foods, etc.

---

## Aperture 1 — Adjacent (Snap KDE template + driver-side reefer attestation)

**Install-base assumptions**

- SOTI T&L customers in cold-chain or food-3PL: estimated 30–80 customers (cold chain is a smaller segment of SOTI's published footprint than trucking). **Internal data dependency.**
- Average sites per customer: 1–8.
- Average reefer drivers (carrier customers) or scanner endpoints per customer: 50–500.
- Total addressable cold-chain endpoints: 5K–30K.

**Attach + ACV**

- Snap FSMA template attach: 30–50% over 18 months (regulatory-driven; high-attention).
- Per-endpoint Snap ACV: $5–$10/endpoint/month → $60–120/endpoint/year.

**Headline math (Aperture 1)**

| Scenario | Endpoints | Attach | ACV/endpoint/yr | Annual revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 5K | 30% (1.5K) | $60 | **~$90K/yr** |
| Base | 15K | 40% (6K) | $90 | **~$540K/yr** |
| Aggressive | 30K | 50% (15K) | $120 | **~$1.8M/yr** |

Aperture 1 alone is modest in revenue but **regulatory-driven attach** makes it the highest-conversion candidate in the pipeline.

## Aperture 2 — Platform-extension (FDA-format export + audit-pack)

**Install-base + prospects**

- Qualified install-base customers (cold-chain): 30–80.
- Adjacent prospects (food 3PLs, reefer carriers reachable via channel): 200–500 NA accounts addressable through trade-press + VAR motion.
- Total addressable customer accounts: 230–580.

**ACV**

- Compliance layer ACV: $20K–$60K/site/year (priced below Trustwell enterprise at $100K+).

**Headline math (Aperture 2)**

| Scenario | Accounts | Win share | ACV/account/yr | Annual revenue (steady-state late 2027) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 230 | 5% (11) | $20K | **~$220K/yr** |
| Base | 400 | 10% (40) | $40K | **~$1.6M/yr** |
| Aggressive | 580 | 20% (116) | $60K | **~$7M/yr** |

The enforcement timing of July 2028 means the steady-state is more like 2028–2029. Pre-enforcement (2026–2027), revenue is much smaller.

---

## Aperture 3 — Net-new SaaS (not recommended)

Trustwell and FoodLogiQ own the food-safety-buyer relationship. SOTI net-new is uncompetitive.

---

## Sensitivity / risks

- **Enforcement timing is the dominant variable.** July 2028 enforcement extension means real demand peaks late 2027. Aperture 1 ship in 2026 is "early" — necessary for credibility, slow on revenue.
- **Internal data dependency:** Cold-chain customer count is the most sensitive input. If SOTI has only 10–20 cold-chain customers, the install-base play is too small.
- **Trustwell consultative motion** is hard to displace; SOTI competes on device-side capture, not consultancy.

---

## Internal data dependencies flagged

- Cold-chain customer count (3PL + reefer carrier)
- Customer-level FSMA 204 readiness (have they bought Trustwell/FoodLogiQ already?)
- Reefer-carrier customer device counts
- FDA-related Snap usage if any
