# 06 — Sizing: POD Evidence-Grade Capture (C4)

## Reference TAM

- Last-mile delivery NA market: ~36–38% of global ~$184B in 2025 → ~$70B NA. Source: [Business Research Company / Technavio](https://www.thebusinessresearchcompany.com/report/last-mile-delivery-global-market-report).
- ePOD software is a small slice of that — directional ~$200–400M NA standalone ePOD; embedded in much wider TMS/last-mile spend.
- Last-mile route optimization software: ~$2B in 2025. Source: [Intel Market Research](https://www.intelmarketresearch.com/last-mile-route-optimization-software-market-42577). ePOD often bundled with routing.

---

## Aperture 1 — Adjacent (Snap multi-shipper POD template)

**Install-base assumptions**

- SOTI T&L customers with last-mile / courier / dry-van POD workflows: estimated 80–200 customers.
- Average drivers per customer: 100–500 (Logistics Provider 3K vehicles is upper bound).
- Addressable drivers in SOTI install base: 30K–150K.

**Attach + ACV**

- Multi-shipper Snap POD attach: 25–45% over 18 months (POD is high-friction today; Snap fit is strong).
- Per-driver Snap POD ACV: $5–$10/driver/month → $60–120/driver/year.

**Headline math (Aperture 1)**

| Scenario | Addressable drivers | Attach | ACV/driver/yr | Annual revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 30K | 25% (7.5K) | $60 | **~$0.5M/yr** |
| Base | 80K | 35% (28K) | $90 | **~$2.5M/yr** |
| Aggressive | 150K | 45% (67K) | $120 | **~$8.0M/yr** |

This is the strongest Aperture 1 economics in the candidate set because the workflow fit is best.

---

## Aperture 2 — Platform-extension (evidence-grade portal + ML)

**Install-base + adjacent-prospect**

- Qualified install-base drivers: 30K–150K (as above) — but only the share where evidence-grade matters (multi-shipper carriers, dispute-heavy verticals): 50–60% of the install base = 15K–90K drivers.
- Adjacent prospects (channel-reachable carriers not on SOTI today): an additional 20K–80K drivers reachable through VAR partnerships.

**ACV**

- Evidence-grade tier ACV: $15–$30/driver/mo → $180–360/driver/yr (premium above Snap baseline).

**Headline math (Aperture 2)**

| Scenario | Drivers | Win share | ACV/driver/yr | Annual revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 30K | 15% (4.5K) | $180 | **~$0.8M/yr** |
| Base | 80K | 25% (20K) | $240 | **~$4.8M/yr** |
| Aggressive | 170K | 35% (60K) | $360 | **~$21.5M/yr** |

Combined Aperture 1 + 2 base case: ~$7M/yr by year 3.

## Aperture 3 — Net-new (not recommended)

NA non-walled-garden last-mile drivers: ~500K+ directional. ePOD standalone competes with Detrack ($25–60/driver/mo). Win share <5% realistic. Reference: 500K × 3% × $480/yr = $7.2M/yr — but requires multi-year separate motion, not recommended.

## Sensitivity / risks

- **Walled-garden share growing:** Amazon DSP + FedEx Ground + Walmart Spark + UPS together cover a growing share of last-mile volume. Non-walled-garden addressable shrinks if this trend continues.
- **Internal data dependency:** Driver count and POD pain by customer.
- **Bringg / Project44 partnership response:** They could move into the SOTI install base via Zebra partnership.
