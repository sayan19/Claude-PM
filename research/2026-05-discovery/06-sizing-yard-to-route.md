# 06 — Sizing: Yard-to-Route Handoff (C6c)

## Reference TAM

- This candidate spans yard ($4.32B), TMS (~$15B+), and visibility (~$3B+) — none cleanly. The TAM frame is misleading because no incumbent products to. The seam pain is real but the budget line doesn't exist as a category.

---

## Aperture 1 — Adjacent (driver notifications + Snap forms)

**Install-base assumptions**

- SOTI T&L customers operating yards AND running route execution (i.e., not pure drop-and-hook): estimated 50–150.
- Total addressable rugged-device endpoints (hostlers + gates + drivers): 20K–80K.

**Attach + ACV**

- Adoption of "yard-to-route notification" Snap module: 15–25% over 18 months.
- Per-endpoint Snap ACV (additive on top of MobiControl): $2–5/endpoint/month → $24–60/endpoint/year.

**Headline math (Aperture 1)**

| Scenario | Endpoints | Attach | ACV/endpoint/yr | Annual revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 20K | 15% (3K) | $24 | **~$72K/yr** |
| Base | 50K | 20% (10K) | $36 | **~$360K/yr** |
| Aggressive | 80K | 25% (20K) | $60 | **~$1.2M/yr** |

Aperture 1 alone is sub-scale.

---

## Aperture 2 — Platform-extension (detention timestamp authority + workflow orchestration)

**This is where the candidate either pays off or doesn't.**

**Assumptions**

- Qualified install-base sites/customers: 50–150 customers, per-customer ACV depends on whether the value prop is "yard ops" (per-site) or "detention recovery" (per-100-trucks).
- Per-customer ACV: $30K–$120K/year (mid-market platform-extension pricing for a workflow product with ROI tied to detention recovery).

**Headline math (Aperture 2)**

| Scenario | Customers | Win share | ACV/customer/yr | Annual revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 50 | 15% (8) | $30K | **~$240K/yr** |
| Base | 100 | 25% (25) | $60K | **~$1.5M/yr** |
| Aggressive | 150 | 35% (52) | $120K | **~$6.3M/yr** |

Sensitivity is huge because the value prop is itself unproven.

---

## Aperture 3 — Net-new SaaS (not recommended)

Loses SOTI's distribution advantage. The whole reason this candidate is interesting is the device-substrate. Net-new packaging eliminates the moat.

---

## Sensitivity / risks

- **Customer signal is the weakest of any candidate.** The pain is real but no budget line exists for it. Sizing is speculative.
- **Build cost is the highest of any candidate** ($4M–$8M Aperture 2). ROI math depends on a base-case of $1.5M/yr → 4–5 year payback at best.
- **The honest read:** size this candidate AFTER customer interviews, not before. Today's math says "do not greenlight without 5+ customer interviews validating funded budget."

---

- Yard-operating customer share
- Detention-cost data from SOTI customers (most won't share without trust)
- Average power units per qualifying customer
