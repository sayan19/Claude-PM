# 05 — SOTI Right-to-Win: POD Evidence-Grade Capture (C4)

## Aperture 1 — Adjacent (extend MobiControl/XSight/Snap)

**Score: 5 / 5**

**Why:**
- POD is the textbook Snap workflow: form, photo, signature, geotag, sync. Multi-shipper configurability is what Snap is built for.
- The driver device is overwhelmingly Zebra rugged Android, MobiControl-managed. The "device + form" stack is exactly SOTI's strongest substrate.
- Build cost is low (months); high-quality template can ship inside two quarters.
- Differentiation against single-tenant ePOD vendors: out-of-the-box multi-shipper config.

**Why not below 5:**
- Photo-quality ML on-device requires real engineering, not Snap configuration. But that's an enhancement, not a blocker.

**Build cost:** Quarters. ~$300K–$700K for a multi-shipper Snap POD template + back-office reporting.
**Distribution:** Existing T&L install base, especially the Logistics Provider, Ruan, K&S customers.
**ACV signal:** $5–$15/driver/mo as Snap-add-on; bundled into MobiControl pricing for install base.

---

## Aperture 2 — Platform-extension (new SOTI capability, MDM-base distribution)

**Score: 4 / 5**

**Why:**
- Evidence-pack auto-assembly + photo-quality ML scoring + back-office dispute portal are platform-extension capabilities beyond Snap.
- Distribution leverages T&L install base.
- Differentiator: "evidence-grade POD" is a positioning angle no MDM peer offers and few ePOD pure-plays have productized.

**Why not 5:**
- Building a back-office portal moves SOTI further from device management toward operations SaaS — buyer-mismatch risk.
- Edge ML for photo coaching is a real engineering investment.
- Bringg, Detrack, Elite EXTRA are not standing still.

**Build cost:** 12–18 months. ~$2M–$4M.
**Distribution:** T&L install base; channel.
**ACV signal:** $15–$30/driver/mo for the evidence-grade tier.

---

## Aperture 3 — Net-new SaaS (standalone)

**Score: 2 / 5**

**Why not:**
- ePOD as a standalone product is a crowded SMB-focused category (Detrack, Track-POD, Onfleet, Bringg).
- Most ePOD purchases bundle with routing/dispatch, where SOTI has no IP.
- Buyer is operations director; SOTI's IT-buyer commercial muscle doesn't transfer.
- Enterprise/big-and-bulky shippers have built or bought already; the addressable mid-market is contested.

**Why not 1:**
- The chain-of-custody niche for medical/legal couriers (Whitespace 5) is real and defensible. But it's a niche, not a standalone bet.

**Build cost:** Multi-year, $10M+
**Distribution:** Direct + channel; separate motion from MDM.
**ACV signal:** $25–$60/driver/mo (matching Detrack et al.).

---

## Aperture verdict

**Lead with Aperture 1 (adjacent) — ship a multi-shipper Snap POD template fast. Layer Aperture 2 (platform-extension) within 12–18 months for evidence-grade capabilities.**

Aperture 1 is the strongest 5/5 score across all candidates because POD is Snap-natural and SOTI's existing T&L customers (Ruan, the Logistics Provider, K&S) are exactly the buyer profile. The bigger strategic question is whether SOTI is willing to grow into the Aperture 2 platform-extension that builds the back-office layer — which crosses into "operations SaaS" territory adjacent to MobiControl's traditional positioning.

Aperture 3 is **not recommended** as a standalone bet.

---

## What needs to be true

1. SOTI's existing T&L customers (especially the Logistics Provider with 3K vehicles, Ruan, K&S) actually have POD as an unsolved or weakly-solved workflow. Validate via interviews — their existing case studies don't tell us this.
2. Multi-shipper configuration is genuinely valued by mid-market regional last-mile carriers (vs. them just using one shipper's mandated app).
3. SOTI is willing to invest in evidence-grade back-office capability beyond Snap.

---

## What would kill this opportunity

- A SOTI customer interview revealing they've already built a custom POD app and don't want SOTI to compete with it.
- Bringg or Project44 launching an ePOD module specifically for the SOTI-managed-device install base via partnership.
- Amazon's continued growth of DSP-mandated stack reducing the addressable non-walled-garden market.

---

## Internal data dependencies flagged

- POD-related Snap usage in T&L customers today
- Customer count by sub-segment (last-mile, courier, big-and-bulky)
- Existing case-study customers' POD workflow status
