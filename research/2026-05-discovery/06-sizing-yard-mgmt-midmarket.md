# 06 — Sizing: Yard Management for Mid-Market 3PLs (C2)

## Reference TAM

- Dock + yard management market: $4.32B in 2025, ~12% CAGR to ~$7.6B by 2030. Source: [Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/dock-yard-management-systems-market-report). NA share ~50% directional → ~$2.2B NA 2025.
- US 3PL market: $323.4B in 2025; 750+ profiled 3PLs in Armstrong's guide. Source: [Armstrong & Associates](https://www.3plogistics.com/3pl-market-info-resources/3pl-market-information/us-3pl-market-size-estimates/), [Armstrong press](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/656921670/armstrong-s-who-s-who-in-logistics-3pl-guide-expands-to-over-750-3pls).

---

## Aperture 1 — Adjacent (Snap hostler/gate UX)

**Install-base assumptions**

- SOTI 3PL/warehousing customers operating yards: estimated 50–150 of total T&L install base (yards are not universal — drop-and-hook carriers don't operate them). **Internal data dependency.**
- Average yard sites per customer: 1–10.
- Total addressable yard sites: 100–500.

**Attach + ACV**

- Snap yard-forms attach: 20–35% over 18 months (those running paper/whiteboard).
- Per-site Snap yard ACV: $2K–$5K/year.

**Headline math (Aperture 1)**

| Scenario | Yard sites | Attach | ACV/site | Annual revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 100 | 20% (20) | $2K | **~$40K/yr** |
| Base | 300 | 25% (75) | $3K | **~$225K/yr** |
| Aggressive | 500 | 35% (175) | $5K | **~$875K/yr** |

Aperture 1 alone is sub-scale. It's a customer-retention defense, not a growth bet.

---

## Aperture 2 — Platform-extension (light YMS + analytics)

**Install-base + adjacent-prospect assumptions**

- Qualified install-base sites: 100–500 (same as above, but stricter "actually want a YMS" filter).
- Adjacent prospects (mid-market 3PLs not yet on SOTI MDM but reachable via channel): ~750+ Armstrong-listed 3PLs × 20% reachable = ~150 channel-addressable accounts × 2–5 sites avg = 300–750 sites.
- Total NA addressable sites for SOTI light-YMS: 400–1,250.

**ACV**

- Light YMS ACV: $20K–$50K/site/year (Velostics/YardView mid-market range).

**Headline math (Aperture 2)**

| Scenario | Addressable sites | Win share (3-yr) | ACV/site | Annual revenue (steady-state) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 400 | 5% (20) | $20K | **~$0.4M/yr** |
| Base | 800 | 10% (80) | $30K | **~$2.4M/yr** |
| Aggressive | 1,250 | 15% (190) | $50K | **~$9.4M/yr** |

Aperture 2 is the only path to meaningful revenue in this candidate.

---

## Aperture 3 — Net-new (not recommended)

Reference: NA mid-market 3PL universe ~750 profiled 3PLs + thousands of long-tail. Even at 5% capture × 5 sites avg × $30K/yr = $5.6M/yr — but this requires multi-year investment in a category SOTI has no brand in. Not recommended.

---

## Sensitivity / risks

- **Internal data dependency:** How many SOTI customers operate yards? The 100–500 range is wide.
- **Build cost is meaningful:** $3M–$8M for Aperture 2 MVP. Steady-state revenue at base case ($2.4M/yr) implies a 2–3 year payback at best.
- **Vector + Velostics consolidation:** if they get to mid-market scale before SOTI ships, the addressable shrinks.
- **Buyer mismatch (operations vs. IT):** SOTI's IT-buyer motion may not close enough deals without a commercial pivot.

---

## Internal data dependencies flagged

- Share of T&L install base operating yards
- Multi-DC customer count
- XSight attach rate in 3PL segment
- Existing partnership relationships with YMS vendors (Vector, Velostics, Kaleris)
