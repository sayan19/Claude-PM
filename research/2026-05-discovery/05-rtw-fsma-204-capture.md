# 05 — SOTI Right-to-Win: FSMA 204 KDE / CTE Capture (C7)

## Aperture 1 — Adjacent (extend MobiControl/XSight/Snap)

**Score: 4 / 5**

**Why:**
- KDE capture at receive/ship is a Snap-natural form: scan lot, capture quantity, capture location, capture date, capture supplier ref, sign.
- Driver-side reefer Snap workflow that bundles temperature attestation + KDE for the Ship event is high-value, low-build.
- The rugged scanner at the cold-storage receiving dock is overwhelmingly Zebra Android, MobiControl-managed.
- Build cost low; ship in quarters.

**Why not 5:**
- A "form that captures KDEs" without an FDA-format export pipeline is incomplete. The form alone is necessary but not sufficient.
- Trustwell/FoodLogiQ exist and are pushing for the same buyer.

**Build cost:** Quarters. ~$300K–$700K for Snap KDE template + driver-side reefer attestation.
**Distribution:** Existing T&L install base in cold-chain and 3PL.
**ACV signal:** $5–$15/asset/mo Snap add-on.

---

## Aperture 2 — Platform-extension

**Score: 4 / 5**

**Why:**
- A SOTI "FSMA 204 capture layer" with FDA-format export, audit-pack assembly, and cross-actor reconciliation extends beyond Snap.
- This becomes a real product wedge — not "we have a form" but "we emit FDA-mandated records from device-captured events."
- Distribution leverages T&L install base in cold-chain.
- Distinctive against Trustwell/FoodLogiQ because it's device-side capture, not shipper-portal-led.

**Why not 5:**
- Compliance officer is the buyer; SOTI's IT-buyer commercial muscle needs adaptation.
- The Aug 2025 FDA enforcement extension to July 2028 reduces near-term urgency — customers may delay.
- Trustwell has consultancy + implementation muscle SOTI doesn't have.

**Build cost:** 12–18 months. ~$2M–$4M for export layer + audit-pack + cross-actor reconciliation.
**Distribution:** T&L install base; food-safety trade press; channel via VARs.
**ACV signal:** $20K–$60K/site/year for the platform layer (priced below Trustwell enterprise).

---

## Aperture 3 — Net-new SaaS

**Score: 2 / 5**

**Why not:**
- Standalone food traceability is Trustwell's category. SOTI has no food-safety brand.
- Compliance officer buyer; long sales cycles with food-safety committees.
- Enforcement extension to 2028 means the buying frenzy SOTI would ride is delayed.

**Why not 1:**
- The carrier-side wedge (Whitespace 1) is real and unowned — but standalone is hard. Better as Aperture 2.

**Build cost:** Multi-year, $10M+
**Distribution:** Direct + channel; food-safety conferences; consultancy partnerships
**ACV signal:** $40K–$150K/site/year (Trustwell-adjacent)

---

## Aperture verdict

**Lead with Aperture 1 (adjacent) for fast SOTI customer protection. Build Aperture 2 (platform-extension) opportunistically as the FSMA 204 enforcement clock approaches July 2028.**

The FDA's enforcement extension to 2028 is the single most important context. It means:
- Aperture 1 ship now is defensive — protect SOTI's existing cold-chain customers from churn-to-Trustwell.
- Aperture 2 build can be paced to the enforcement curve. By late 2027, panic-buying creates demand.
- Aperture 3 is not the right bet — Trustwell has the consultative compliance brand and SOTI doesn't.

The asymmetry: this candidate has the strongest **regulatory tailwind** of any candidate, but the slowest-burning timer. Other candidates may be more urgent on a 12–24 month horizon.

---

## What needs to be true

1. SOTI has cold-chain customers in the T&L install base (Lineage, Americold, US Cold Storage, regional 3PLs, reefer carriers). Validate via internal CRM.
2. Reefer-carrier customers are a meaningful share of the cold-chain footprint.
3. Customer interviews validate that compliance officer / VP food safety would buy device-side KDE capture vs. building it custom.

---

## What would kill this opportunity

- Trustwell launches a Zebra-validated capture template aimed at the SOTI install base.
- FDA further extends or weakens FSMA 204.
- A WMS vendor (Manhattan, Korber) ships a polished KDE capture module that obviates wrap-around solutions.

- Reefer-carrier customer count

- Existing food-safety-related Snap usage if any
