## 1. Executive verdict

Five candidate workflow opportunities survived Phase 2 deep-dives. The honest read across the three apertures:

- **Ship now (Aperture 1, no-regrets):** A multi-shipper Snap **POD evidence template** (C4) and a Snap **DVIR + roadside-prep template** (C1). Both are months-to-build, attach to existing T&L install base, and protect SOTI customers from churn to Whip Around / Detrack / Bringg as those vendors push into the rugged-Android footprint. Combined Aperture 1 base case: ~$5M/yr by year 2.
- **Strategic platform-extension build (Aperture 2):** **POD evidence-grade portal** + **DVIR compliance layer** (CSA + roadside-prep) on the install base. 12–18 month builds; combined steady-state base case ~$8M–$10M/yr. These are where SOTI's distinctive position (device substrate + MDM-grade trust) translates into a moat no MDM peer can match.
- **Highest-distinctiveness, highest-uncertainty bet:** **Yard-to-route handoff** (C6c, the seeded bundle hypothesis's most novel seam). The whitespace is the most empty in the entire candidate set, but customer signal is the weakest. **Do NOT greenlight without 5+ customer interviews validating a funded budget.** If interviews confirm pain + budget, this becomes the strategic Aperture 2 bet for 2027.
- **Regulatory-tailwind opportunistic build:** **FSMA 204 KDE/CTE capture** (C7) on the FDA 2028 enforcement curve. Ship Aperture 1 Snap template in 2026 to defend cold-chain customers; pace Aperture 2 build to enforcement clock.
- **Validate-then-decide:** **Mid-market 3PL yard management** (C2). Customer-validation interviews needed; the operations-buyer commercial pivot is real friction.
- **Drop or defer:** Net-new SaaS bets (Aperture 3) on every candidate. The category competition is too intense and SOTI's distribution advantage evaporates outside the install base.

**Recommendation in one sentence:** Greenlight POD (C4) and DVIR (C1) Aperture 1 templates immediately; run a 6-week customer interview sprint on yard-to-route (C6c) and yard mgmt (C2) before committing Aperture 2 build budgets; ship FSMA 204 (C7) Aperture 1 template by Q4 2026 to defend cold-chain.

## 4. Top 3 opportunities

### Opportunity 1: POD evidence-grade capture (C4)

**The right-to-win argument.** POD is the single textbook Snap workflow: form, photo, signature, geotag, sync to back-office. SOTI's existing T&L customers (Ruan ~5K devices, Logistics Provider 3K vehicles, K&S Couriers per published case studies) all have POD as a workflow — but the multi-shipper configurability gap, the photo-quality gap, and the dispute-pack assembly gap leave value on the table that Detrack, Track-POD, and Onfleet don't fully close. SOTI is the only player at the intersection of (a) device-management trust, (b) Snap form-building substrate, and (c) edge-ML-on-rugged-Android capability. This is the highest 5/5 Aperture 1 score in the candidate set.

**The bet.**
- **Aperture 1:** Multi-shipper Snap POD template + photo-quality validation. Ship Q3 2026. Build: $300K–$700K. Base-case revenue ~$2.5M/yr by year 2.
- **Aperture 2:** Evidence-grade dispute-pack portal + on-device photo-quality ML. Ship by Q4 2027. Build: $2M–$4M. Base-case revenue ~$4.8M/yr at steady state.

**Aperture honesty:** This is an attach + platform-extension play. It is NOT a net-new SaaS — Detrack, Onfleet, Bringg own that category and SOTI's distribution advantage is the install base, not direct sales.

**Why it wins:** Existing customer-relationship leverage; lowest-risk Aperture 1 in the set; defends against ePOD vendor incursion into the rugged-Android footprint.

**Reference:** 03-competitive-pod-evidence.md, 04-whitespace-pod-evidence.md, 05-rtw-pod-evidence.md, 06-sizing-pod-evidence.md.

### Opportunity 2: DVIR + roadside-inspection prep (C1)

**The right-to-win argument.** DVIR is the second-cleanest Snap fit. The Aperture 2 distinctive bet — combining DVIR with FMCSA SafeStat-fed CSA-score visibility and roadside-inspection-prep at the moment of pull-over — is something no MDM peer (Workspace ONE, Intune, Hexnode) is positioned to build, AND no DVIR pure-play (Whip Around, Fleetio, Zonar) builds because they don't sit at the device-management substrate. The FMCSA Final Rule (Docket FMCSA-2025-0115, effective March 23, 2026) formalizing eDVIR acceptance accelerates the digital pull. Source: [Heavy Vehicle Inspection / FMCSA docket](https://heavyvehicleinspection.com/article/top-fleet-inspection-software-comparison-features-pricing).

**The bet.**
- **Aperture 1:** Snap DVIR template (defect routing + photo evidence + maintenance handoff). Ship Q3 2026. Build: $200K–$500K. Base-case revenue ~$2.9M/yr by year 2.
- **Aperture 2:** Compliance layer — CSA score visibility + roadside-prep + cross-system reconciliation for mixed-fleet carriers. Ship Q1 2028. Build: $2M–$5M. Base-case revenue ~$3.2M/yr steady state.

**Aperture honesty:** Aperture 1 alone is defensive (ELD-bundled DVIR is "good enough free" for many carriers). Aperture 2 is where the differentiation lives. Aperture 3 standalone is a no-go — Whip Around won the SMB no-hardware niche, Zonar owns the patent-protected verifiable-inspection premium.

**Why it wins:** Regulatory tailwind, install-base distribution, no MDM peer competing.

**Reference:** 03/04/05/06 dvir-compliance artifacts.

### Opportunity 3: Yard-to-route handoff (C6c) — *contingent on customer validation*

**The right-to-win argument.** This is the most distinctive whitespace in the entire candidate set. No incumbent owns the seam between yard exit and route execution — FourKites Dynamic Yard is closest but is shipper-paid enterprise visibility, leaving carrier-side and driver-side execution unaddressed. SOTI's substrate (rugged devices managed by MobiControl across hostler, gate guard, and driver) is *the only common substrate across all three actors*. Productizing SOTI-device-captured timestamps as audit-grade detention evidence is a wedge no one else can build the same way.

**The bet (contingent).**
- **Aperture 1:** Driver-side delay-aware notifications + Snap event capture forms. Ship Q4 2026. Build: ~$300K. Base-case revenue ~$360K/yr (modest standalone).
- **Aperture 2:** Detention timestamp authority + cross-system workflow orchestration + carrier-priced visibility. 18–24 month build. Build: $4M–$8M. Base-case revenue ~$1.5M/yr (steady state). Aggressive case ~$6M/yr.

**Aperture honesty:** Customer signal is weakest of any candidate — the seam pain is real but no funded buyer line exists today. **Recommendation: do NOT greenlight Aperture 2 build without ≥5 mid-market carrier interviews confirming detention recovery as a CFO-level pain with budget intent.**

**Why it could win:** Most distinctive theoretical position. **Why it might not:** Pain may be operational annoyance, not budget line. Vector or Velostics could close the seam first.

**Reference:** 03/04/05/06 yard-to-route artifacts.

## 5. What we still don't know — Customer interview agenda

This is the single most important section. The biggest documented v1 failure was using market data as a substitute for customer signal. Every "directional market signal" in this brief needs to convert to "validated customer signal" before Aperture 2 builds get committed.

### Cohort 1: SOTI's existing T&L flagship customers (3–5 interviews)
**Targets:** Ruan, the unnamed Logistics Provider (per published case study), K&S Couriers, iDC Logistics, and the largest 2 cold-chain customers from internal CRM.
**Questions:**
1. Walk me through your DVIR workflow today. Where does it break? Have you considered Whip Around / Fleetio?
2. How do you handle multi-shipper POD requirements? What does dispute defense cost you?
3. Do you operate yards? What's your hostler / gate-guard tech stack? Is the device UX painful?
4. (Cold chain only) How are you preparing for FSMA 204? Have you bought Trustwell or FoodLogiQ?
5. How do you reconcile yard-side timing with route plans? Is detention recovery a CFO-level pain?
6. If SOTI shipped a Snap-based [POD / DVIR / KDE] template tomorrow, would you pilot it? At what price?

### Cohort 2: SOTI mid-market T&L prospects (5–7 interviews)
**Targets:** mid-market trucking (50–500 power units), regional last-mile carriers, mid-market 3PLs operating yards. Recruit through VAR partners.
**Questions:** same as Cohort 1, plus competitive context — what made them NOT buy SOTI before?

### Cohort 3: Cold-chain compliance officers (3–4 interviews)
**Targets:** VP Food Safety / Compliance Officer at cold-storage 3PLs and reefer carriers (1 currently using Trustwell, 2 not yet on a traceability platform, 1 large reefer carrier).
**Questions:**
1. What's your FSMA 204 plan today? Where are you in the maturity curve?
2. The July 2028 enforcement extension — has it changed your buying timing?
3. Would device-side KDE capture (Snap form on the rugged scanner) be useful, or do you need a full platform?
4. Reefer carriers specifically: are you tooled for the Ship CTE? What would you pay for a clean tool?

### Cohort 4: Yard-to-route validation (5+ interviews — required gate for C6c)
**Targets:** Operations Director at mid-market 3PLs and carriers (200–500 power units, multi-DC).
**Questions:**
1. Walk me through what happens between "load departed dock 7" and "driver picks up at the gate." How long? Whose time gets wasted?
2. What's your monthly detention recovery vs. detention exposure? Where do you lose disputes?
3. If we offered a tool that gave you audit-grade timestamps for detention disputes, would the CFO fund it? At what price?
4. Have you used FourKites Dynamic Yard? What's the gap?
5. Is the yard-to-route handoff a budget line in your operations P&L, or an operational annoyance?

### What the answers must produce
- Validation or invalidation of each candidate's customer signal.
- A short list of 2–3 design-partner customers per candidate.
- Pricing reality check (vs. the speculative ACV ranges in the sizing artifacts).
- Decision input for the May–June 2026 build prioritization meeting.

## 6. Risks and counter-arguments

### What would kill POD (C4)
- A SOTI customer interview revealing they've already built a custom POD app and don't want SOTI competing. (Probability: medium.)
- Bringg or Project44 launching an ePOD module specifically for the SOTI install base via Zebra partnership. (Probability: medium.)
- Walled-garden share (Amazon DSP, FedEx, UPS, Walmart) growing faster than expected, shrinking addressable market. (Probability: medium-high.)

### What would kill DVIR (C1)
- Motive or Samsara expanding into MDM-adjacent device management and bundling DVIR + lite-MDM defensively. (Probability: medium — Samsara is the more likely actor.)
- Brady Corp post-Honeywell ($1.4B sale) entering device-management + workflow software. (Probability: low-medium near-term.)
- A VAR-channel-led Whip Around marketing push into the SOTI install base. (Probability: medium.)

### What would kill yard-to-route (C6c)
- Customer interviews reveal pain is real but no budget line. (Probability: medium-high — this is the central risk.)
- FourKites pushes down-market with carrier-priced visibility. (Probability: medium.)
- Vector or Velostics ships a Zebra-validated yard+driver bundle. (Probability: medium.)
- TMS vendor (McLeod, MercuryGate) builds the seam as a free module. (Probability: low-medium.)

### What would kill FSMA 204 (C7)
- FDA further extends or weakens FSMA 204 beyond July 2028. (Probability: low-medium — political pressure exists.)
- Trustwell launches a Zebra-validated capture template aimed at SOTI's install base. (Probability: medium.)
- WMS vendor (Manhattan, Korber) ships polished KDE capture obviating wrap-around. (Probability: medium-high.)

### What would kill yard mgmt (C2)
- A SOTI customer interview revealing yards aren't a meaningful share of the install base. (Probability: medium — internal CRM should answer this fast.)
- Vector + Velostics consolidation faster than SOTI ships. (Probability: high.)

### Cross-cutting risks
- **MobiControl modernization budget pressure.** Any of these candidates competes with core MobiControl/XSight/Snap roadmap. Be honest about which roadmap items get deprioritized.
- **Channel motion shift.** Long-tail SMB carrier reach requires VAR / MSP / insurance-broker channels SOTI may not have invested in fully.
- **Hexnode / Workspace ONE / Intune attack on T&L install base.** Defensive Aperture 1 plays matter regardless of growth-thesis, because they protect the base.

## 9. Closing note — what this rebuild fixed vs. v1

For the record, this v2 brief addressed each of the v1 failure modes:

1. **MDM-bounded competitive scans → category-deep scans.** Each of the five 03-competitive-* artifacts covers ELD-bundled, pure-plays, TMS/WMS-bundled, OEM-bundled, status quo — not just MDM peers.
2. **TAM-as-decision-driver → install-base × attach × ACV.** Each 06-sizing-* artifact leads with the install-base math and treats TAM as reference.
3. **Overclaiming → validation honesty.** Every "directional market signal" is labeled distinct from "validated customer signal." Customer-interview agenda is explicit.
4. **MobiControl backgrounded → MobiControl as anchor.** Every Aperture 1 / 2 RTW score names MobiControl as the platform foundation; Snap and XSight layer on top.
5. **Geographic drift → NA-only.** FMCSA, FDA FSMA 204, DOT are the regulatory anchors throughout. Where global TAMs were used (last-mile market, FSM market), NA carve-outs were stated.
6. **Customer-signal hand-waving → explicit customer-interview agenda.** Section 5 of this brief lists every claim that needs interview validation and the cohort that should validate it.

The most important structural fix: every candidate was scored against all three apertures, and the verdict is honest about which aperture each candidate fits — including saying "Aperture 3 net-new SaaS is not recommended" five times where it's true, even when net-new looks more glamorous.
