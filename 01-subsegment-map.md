## Detailed Profile: Last-Mile and Final-Mile Delivery

**What this sub-segment is:**
Companies that move packages from a depot or distribution center to the end customer's door. This includes parcel carriers, e-commerce fulfillment providers, and postal services. The segment runs on high volumes of daily stops, tight delivery windows, and proof-of-delivery capture.

**SOTI's presence today:**
Strong. SOTI has published case studies for multiple last-mile delivery customers. TCC (Transportadora Comercial Colombia), a package transport company making more than 36,000 deliveries daily, saved 400 working hours per month and over $108K USD annually using MobiControl.
Source: [SOTI TCC Case Study](https://soti.net/resources/customer-stories-and-use-cases/tcc/)

DPD Germany, the second largest international parcel delivery company in Europe with 78 depots, 9,500 employees, and 11,000 drivers, uses MobiControl to push approximately 48,000 app updates per year.
Source: [SOTI DPD Case Study](https://soti.net/media/1460/en_dpd_jan2015.pdf)

Aramex uses MobiControl in its global operations. Blue Dart and Bpost are also documented SOTI customers.

Source: [SOTI Delivery Hero Case Study](https://soti.net/resources/case-studies/delivery-hero/)

## Detailed Profile: LTL and FTL Trucking and Freight

**What this sub-segment is:**
Companies that move freight over the road, either as full truckloads (FTL) or as partial loads consolidated with other shipments (LTL). Drivers use mobile devices for electronic logging (ELD), route navigation, proof-of-delivery, and vehicle inspections. The FMCSA ELD mandate makes in-cab technology a compliance requirement, not optional.

**SOTI's presence today:**
Moderate. Ruan, a transportation management company, deployed MobiControl to remotely provision and troubleshoot driver devices, eliminating the need to ship devices to IT. This saved approximately $60,000 USD annually. ## Detailed Profile: Warehousing and Distribution Centers (3PL Operators)

**What this sub-segment is:**
Third-party logistics providers that operate warehouses and distribution centers on behalf of other companies. Workers use mobile devices for picking, receiving, put-away, cycle counting, and shipping. Devices are typically shared across shifts, creating unique management challenges around user profiles, battery rotation, and hygiene.

**SOTI's presence today:**
Strong. iDC Logistics, a 3PL serving carriers across consumer electronics, industrial products, food and beverage, and general retail, deployed SOTI MobiControl XS (MobiControl plus XSight plus Premium Plus Service). Before SOTI, iDC had no visibility into devices and excessive downtime was a constant challenge. After deployment, device troubleshooting takes one hour or less and device configuration takes just 10 minutes. iDC uses XSight Battery Dashboards, App Usage Dashboards, and Remote Control.
Source: [SOTI iDC Logistics Case Study](https://soti.net/resources/customer-stories-and-use-cases/idc-logistics/?csc=)

A leading logistics provider handling over one million packages daily with thousands of employees and a fleet of over 3,000 vehicles implemented MobiControl XS, including Battery Dashboards to predict which batteries will last an entire shift.
Source: [SOTI Logistics Provider Use Case](https://soti.net/resources/customer-stories-and-use-cases/logistics-provider/)

SOTI published a blog in January 2026 on MDM benefits in the warehouse, referencing integration with warehouse management systems and operational gains.

## Detailed Profile: Field Service Logistics

Companies that dispatch technicians or service workers to customer sites for installation, repair, maintenance, or reverse logistics (returns, parts recovery). Devices are used for work order management, parts lookup, service documentation, and customer sign-off. This segment overlaps with T&L because it involves vehicle fleets, dispatch, and route optimization.

**SOTI Road Ahead 2024 report (T&L-specific data):**
Research conducted May to June 2024 across 10 countries, 1,700 interviews with T&L drivers and warehouse workers at organizations with 50+ employees. 98% of respondents use mobile technology daily. Workers lose an average of 13 hours per month to device-related downtime. 35% work overtime due to delays. 1 in 3 drivers miss targets because of device challenges. 29% of delivery drivers admit to speeding to compensate for delays. 48% of workers report stress from device downtime. Canada: 35 hours per month lost to downtime. Australia: 16 hours per month. UK: 11 hours per month.
Source: [SOTI Road Ahead Newsroom](https://soti.net/resources/newsroom/2024/soti-research-reveals-digital-dilemmas-in-the-transportation-and-logistics-industry/)
Source: [Logistics IT Magazine](https://www.logisticsit.com/articles/2024/10/08/soti-research-transport-and-logistics-sector%E2%80%99s-digital-future-hindered-by-11-hours-of-downtime-per-month)

**Digital logistics market:** Projected to expand from approximately USD 37.6 billion in 2025 to over USD 120 billion by 2032.
